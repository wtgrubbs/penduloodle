About Penduloodle

Pendulum painting is a mesmerizing art form where a paint bucket is suspended from a cord and set in swinging motion. As paint streams from a hole in the bottom, the non-linear physics of 3D spherical pendulum motion creates intricate, swirling Spirograph geometric patterns on a canvas below.

Penduloodle simulates this physics-based art using real Newtonian dynamics, but with a unique twist: the Canvas itself is attached to an independent pendulum! This dual-pendulum setup offers remarkable flexibility and complexity for generating digital art, appealing to those with a joint interest in physics and creative expression.

If you are unfamiliar with this artform, we highly recommend doing an internet search for "Pendulum Painting Videos" to witness real artists practicing it in action!

Controls Guide

Adjust these parameters to experiment with different physical forces and achieve unique patterns.

  • Length: Sets the physical length of the pendulum cord. Longer cords produce wider, slower swings.
  • Gravity & Damping: Controls gravitational pull and air resistance. Higher damping causes the pendulum to lose energy and settle toward the center faster.
  • Initial X / Initial Y: Sets the starting spatial coordinates. Swings are realistically constrained so the pendulum doesn't flip.
  • Velocity X / Velocity Y: Applies an initial directional push to the pendulum at the exact moment the simulation begins.

Tool Bar Options:

  • Speed & Quality: Adjusts playback speed and mathematically scales the Canvas resolution (higher quality offers smoother outputs but requires more CPU power).
  • Rainbow: Toggles a mode where the pen color smoothly cycles through the entire color spectrum as it swings.
